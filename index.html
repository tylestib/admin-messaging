Content Guidelines

✖ Prohibited Content

  • References to Amazon competitors or off-Amazon purchases
  • Prohibited PII (personal addresses, SSNs, personal phone numbers, non-work emails)
  • Inaccurate information about Amazon Business services

✔ Approved Content

  • Company purchasing policies and preferences
  • Internal contact information (Admin/Category Manager)
  • Product category guidance aligned with contracts
  • Process requirements for different purchase types

    ⏱ ProServe will contact you when your access is ready. SLA: 48 hour turnaround.
  2. Navigate to the Guided Buying Console and select the Admin Messaging tab.
  3. Enter the BAID (Business Account ID).
  4. Enter the customer message. Tip: Use Kiro/Quick to format the message in Markdown.
  5. Publish the message.

What Is Admin Messaging?

Admin Messaging is a feature that enables your organization's purchasing administrators to communicate important policies, guidance, and updates directly to buyers within Amazon Business.

This ensures that everyone in your organization stays informed about purchasing procedures, approved categories, spending thresholds, and key contactsÃ¢â‚¬â€without relying on external emails or separate communication channels.

How It Works

  1. When a new message is posted, it appears as a popup notification the first time a buyer visits the Amazon Business homepage.
  2. After the initial viewing, buyers can revisit the message at any time through the Business Notification Panel.
  3. If the message content is updated by your administrator, the popup will reappear to ensure all buyers see the latest information.

No action is required by buyers beyond reviewing the message. The notification is informational and designed to keep your team aligned with current purchasing policies.

What You May See

The Amazon Business team works with your account administrators to craft messaging tailored to your organization. Messages may include:

  • Company purchasing policies and preferences
  • Preferred or restricted product categories
  • Spending thresholds and approval requirements
  • Internal contact information for procurement questions
  • Guidance on purchase order or PCard procedures

All message content is created and maintained by the Amazon Business team in collaboration with your account administrators to ensure accuracy and relevance.

FAQs

What is the character limit?

The character limit for Admin Messages is 1,000 characters. Note: spaces and Markdown syntax (e.g., **, #, []()), all count as characters, so factor that into your message length.

What are the known limitations?
  • Links embedded in the message will open in the same tab. We are currently testing to see if we can get links to open in a new tab.
  • Images and logos are not supported.
What is the process for the pilot?

We are creating a SIM managed by ProServe. You will create the SIM and enter all required information (CID, BAID, proposed messaging). The SIM will be reviewed and approved by ProServe. Once approved, you will have access to the Guided Buying Console for that BAID.


⏱ SLA: 48 hours to approve the SIM.
What happens when the CSM/SAM is no longer supporting the customer?

The customer will be provided with a one-pager on Admin Messaging and directions to work with their Account Executive to make modifications. The Account Executive will submit a SIM for the modifications.

How are the messages being monitored?

The Guided Buying Team has access to the logs and can audit all activity in the console.

Can the admin make the changes themselves (self-service)?

No. Self-service editing by customer admins is not currently available. We are planning to add this capability to the roadmap, but at this time all changes are managed through the Guided Buying Console by ProServe or SAMs.

How do I discuss legal guidelines with my customer?

Amazon Business takes privacy and security very seriously. We want to ensure that we operate with everyone's best interest in mind, so we need to ensure the guardrails are in place. If you feel there is a valid exception, please open a SIM for review.

Are there any country-specific requirements?

Yes. Per Legal, Admin Messages in Japan and Mexico must begin with:

"The following is a message from [AB customer name]"

Note: Japan is included in the pilot. Mexico and other countries will be included in the worldwide rollout.
